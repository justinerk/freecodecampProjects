Introduction
Hi, how are you? my name is [________].
This is a classic example of an introduction when you are meeting someone. Your name goes in the blank. This is a Class I Interaction, which is discussed more in detail below.
Class I Responses
Interactions falling in Class I are commonly referred to by humans as "smalltalk". As a human person, you must master some basic responses to common queries and statements in Class I.
import human.interactions.classI; if (human.getStatement == "What's up?") { human.respondTo("Good, how are you?"); //Good is used regardless of the speaker's true feelings } if (human.getStatement == "What's up?") { human.respondTo("Not much, how about you?"); } if (human.getStatement == "Bye!" || human.getStatement == "Goodbye!") { human.respondTo("Bye, have a good " + currentTime); }
Class II Responses
import human.interactions.classII;

Class II Interactions are slightly more varied and meaningful and varied than the smalltalk outlined in Class I.

Remember, do not get too specific about anything or you might make an error that could arouse suspicion.

Never imply or hint in any way that you are anything other than human.

But not to worry, we have imported a database including hundreds of Class II interactions.

These types of interactions, though less predictable, cannot be avoided if you are truly to pass as human. In addition to the database, remember these methods to avoid suspicion.

  • Tell humans that you relate to their experiences when they speak of their lives.
  • Engage in charitable or polite gestures towards humans, such as offering assistance towards a human performing a task of any difficulty, or bringing baked goods to a workplace.
  • Complain occaisionally. This is an important activity for humans even if no goal is achieved.
  • Do not speak with perfect grammar or pronunciation 100% of the time. Humans are prone to errors and often shorten or mispronounce words for their convenience.
Class III Responses
import human.interactions.classIII;

Class III Interactions are highly varied and unpredictable, often between humans who have developed a bond with each other. They may share emotions or recall memories to entertain their peers.

Proper responses to Class III Interactions are highly nuanced and although we have implemented a database, there is a high risk of encountering unscripted interactions and making an error that could potentially breach your identity.

Avoid these interactions as much as possible. Silence is better than an identity breach.

Assessing Risk Of Breach

This section outlines what to do in case of a suspected identity breach, and if Identity Breach Protocol must be activated.

If you believe your identity has been breached, do not act immediately.

If any of the following are true, you may need to perform an Identity Breach Risk Assessment Protocol

  • Humans have suddenly changed their behavior around you to be more avoidant.
  • You have engaged in Class III interactions with a human and potentially aroused suspicion.
  • A human has made physical contact with your skin.
  • A human has witnessed or was present for a malfunction or reboot.
  • A human has witnessed or was present while you were in communication with [redacted].
Identity Breach Protocol

For a minor breach (Class I), a simple relocation will suffice.

contacts.delete(prevLoc); places.add(newLoc); contacts.add(newLoc);
